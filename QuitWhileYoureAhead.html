Quit while you are ahead

Study of different blackjack betting systems.

First things first

Before knowing how we are going to bet we have to figure out how we are going to play. For the gathered data the playing strategy is a simplified version of the 4/6/8 decks method where dealer stands on all 17. The playstyle was kept as simple as possible to try and give more importance to the bets themselves.


About the betting systems

Theres a lot of options out there, here are some of the best known ones, but are these the best ones? Choose wisely


Flat-Betting

As simple as it comes, always bet the same amount of money, no matter the result of the previous hand.


Positive-Progression

If you win, up your wager by 0.5 times your Bet Unit. If you lose, reset your wager.


Negative-Progression

If you lose, up your wager by 0.5 times your Bet Unit. If you win, reset your wager.


Double-Down

Works like Positive-Progression but whenever you win you double your last wager.


Martingale

Based on Negative-Progression, but you double your wager when you lose and keep it the same when you win.


Oscar

This is a tricky one, the main idea of Oscar's method is to win a single Bet Unit after a series of bets, after this you start a new series of wagers. To accomplish this your wagers are increased by 1 unit if you win until you get your money back +1 unit, when you should start a new series. Losing keeps your wager the same.


Let's get down to business!

All this systems were put to test in 3 rounds of 100 hands each (or until money is exhausted).

About the games

The different betting systems benefit from different scenarios, for example, Negative-Progression can take advantage of a losing streak provided its not too long. However, in the end we all agree that the more wins we get the better and, unfortunately, these rounds left a bit to be desired. Narrowing it down...

Seeing the results, we could say a thing or two about the betting systems. For starters both Martingale and Double-Down use up all your cash before your 20th hand in every round. Yes, they do award high rewards provided you stop playing at the right time, but the risk is a bit too high.

Both Positive-Progression and Negative-Progression show similar behaviours despite the fact that the latter busted in one of the rounds.

Taking a look at Flat-Betting, the fact that it is the simplest one makes the earnings kind of meh, but then again, its easy to pull off and the risks are as low as they get.

At last, but most certaintly not the least, we have Oscar's method, even though the earnings are not the highest, its the only method in which you, at least, get all your money back by the end of the round in every round, and thats pretty impressive.
